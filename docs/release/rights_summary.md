# Rights Summary

## Source Policy

The corpus is public-domain-first. Each usable text version should have source metadata and a license/public-domain rationale.

## Current License Status

From current reports:

- `public_domain_or_gutenberg_terms`
- `public_domain`
- `public_domain_us`
- `per_item_review_required`

Open Library and Wikisource records are source-resolution leads unless their underlying rights/source details are pinned.

## OCR Policy

- `reject`: skipped during normalization.
- `ocr_bronze`: retained only as retrieval-only and excluded from training export.
- `ocr_silver` and `ocr_gold`: eligible subject to source rights and provenance.

## JSTOR Note

JSTOR Early Journal Content may be useful for discovery. Do not treat JSTOR text-analysis access as training permission unless legal review approves it.

## Release Caveat

This summary is not legal advice. Before public release, re-check source URLs, edition metadata, translator/editor dates, and any per-item review records.
